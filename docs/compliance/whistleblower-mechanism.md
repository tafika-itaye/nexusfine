# Whistle-Blower Mechanism

**Status:** v1.0 · **Issued:** 2026-05-16
**Owner:** TechNexus Compliance function · **Authority:** Board of Directors
**Applies to:** TechNexus employees, contractors, partners, sub-suppliers, and
any third party connected to the NexusFine programme.

This policy is a **required procurement-trust artefact**. Public-sector
RFIs in Malawi, Zambia, Mozambique and Tanzania routinely require
suppliers to evidence an active reporting channel for bribery,
corruption, fraud or misconduct.

---

## 1. What may be reported

Any of the following, suspected on reasonable grounds:

- Bribery or attempted bribery, given or received, in any direction
- Solicitation of facilitation payments by an official
- Misappropriation of funds, assets, or data
- Fraud — invoicing, time-keeping, or procurement-related
- Unauthorised disclosure of citizen data
- Discrimination, harassment, or workplace abuse
- Falsification of records (audit log, fines issued, payments)
- Procurement-process irregularities (bid leakage, collusion)
- Endangerment of life, health, or public safety
- Knowing breach of the Data Protection Act, 2024
- Retaliation against a prior whistleblower

Disagreement with a business decision is **not** in scope. Use the
ordinary feedback / grievance channel for that.

---

## 2. How to report

There are **three channels**. All three reach the same Compliance Officer
within one business day:

| Channel | How |
|---------|-----|
| **Email** | `whistleblower@technexusmw.com` — encrypted at rest; only the Compliance Officer + an alternate Director can read it. |
| **Phone / WhatsApp** | `+265 9XX XXX XXX` — answered by the Compliance Officer in person; voicemail goes to a separate locked mailbox. |
| **Physical post** | Confidential. To: Compliance Officer, TechNexus MW, P.O. Box ___, Blantyre. Sealed envelope marked "CONFIDENTIAL — DO NOT OPEN". |

Reports may be **anonymous** or **named**. Anonymous reports are treated
with the same diligence; only the case-handling team knows it is
anonymous.

Reports may also be made to the **Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB)** at
`hotline@acb.mw` or +265 1 770 011, or to the **Office of the Director of
Public Prosecutions**. Reporting to ACB or DPP does not affect the
reporter's rights at TechNexus and is sometimes the right route — for
example, when the alleged conduct involves senior leadership.

---

## 3. What the reporter is promised

1. **No retaliation.** Any adverse action against the reporter is itself
   misconduct, regardless of whether the underlying report is upheld.
   This applies to reports made in good faith, even if the substance
   turns out not to be true.
2. **Confidentiality.** The reporter's identity (if known) is shared only
   with the Compliance Officer, the appointed investigator, and an
   alternate Director. Where the matter is referred to ACB or police, the
   reporter is told **before** any disclosure is made unless the law
   requires otherwise.
3. **Acknowledgement within 3 business days.** Where the reporter is
   contactable.
4. **Update within 30 days** on the status (open / closed / referred).
5. **Right to bring a representative** to any meeting.
6. **Right to redress** if retaliation occurs — including reinstatement,
   compensation, and corrective disciplinary action against the
   retaliator.

## 4. What happens after a report

```
   Receive          Triage           Investigate         Decision         Close
     ▼                ▼                  ▼                  ▼              ▼
  ┌────────┐    ┌────────┐         ┌─────────────┐    ┌────────────┐  ┌────────┐
  │ Email/ │ →  │ Compl. │ →       │ Investigator│  → │ Compliance │ →│ Notify │
  │ phone/ │    │ Officer│         │ assigned    │    │ Committee  │  │ reporter│
  │ post   │    │ logs   │         │ (independent│    │ decides    │  │ + close│
  └────────┘    │ ticket │         │  of subject)│    │ outcome    │  └────────┘
                └────────┘         └─────────────┘    └────────────┘
```

### 4.1 Receive
Any channel arrives in a sealed inbox the Compliance Officer reads daily.

### 4.2 Triage (≤ 3 business days)
- Open a case file with a non-sequential ticket ID.
- Assess urgency (life-safety → immediate; fraud → 14-day; misconduct → 30-day).
- If the subject of the report is the Compliance Officer themselves, the
  report routes automatically to the alternate Director.

### 4.3 Investigate
- An investigator is appointed who has no operational reporting line to
  the subject and no declared conflict of interest (see
  `conflict-of-interest-register.md`).
- For matters touching procurement or government interaction, the
  investigator may be external (typically a local law firm under retainer).
- Interview, document review, system-log review. Always with two-person
  witness for any interview.

### 4.4 Decision
- The Compliance Committee (CEO, CTO, alternate Director) reviews the
  investigator's report. The CEO recuses if the subject is in their
  direct reporting line.
- Possible outcomes:
  - **Not substantiated** — close, communicate to reporter.
  - **Substantiated, internal corrective action** — disciplinary,
    process change, training.
  - **Substantiated, referral to authorities** — ACB, DPP, police, MACRA.
  - **Insufficient evidence, monitoring** — keep open, re-test in 90 days.

### 4.5 Close
- Reporter receives a written closure note (where contactable).
- Case file retained for **10 years** at HQ, audit-log-immutable storage.

---

## 5. Specific protections — anti-retaliation

Retaliation includes (non-exhaustive):
- Termination, demotion, or change of role
- Removal from the NexusFine project
- Withholding of training, references, or expected promotion
- Hostile or undermining behaviour by colleagues or managers
- Disclosure of the reporter's identity to anyone outside the case-handling team
- Adverse external comment to a third party (e.g., a future employer)

Any of these → an automatic separate investigation, treated at P1 severity.

---

## 6. Annual reporting

The Compliance Officer publishes an annual report to the Board (and on
request to the client Ministry) containing:

- Total reports received (with severity bands)
- Substantiated / not / inconclusive
- Referrals made to ACB, DPP, MACRA
- Number of retaliation complaints
- Mean time-to-close
- Themes / repeat patterns

The report contains **no identifying information** about reporters or
named individuals — only aggregate.

## 7. Training & awareness

- All new hires complete a 30-minute training within 30 days of joining.
- Existing staff refresh annually.
- The policy is on the staff intranet, in printed form on the office
  notice-board, and laminated on every TechNexus-issued laptop.
- A 1-pager "How to raise a concern" card is in every officer-training
  pack at every police station we deploy.
